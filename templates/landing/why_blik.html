The Three Broken Promises of SaaS Performance Platforms

After analyzing hundreds of user reviews across Lattice, Culture Amp, 15Five, and others, we found the same three failures repeated everywhere.

❌ Promise #1: "Your Feedback Is Anonymous"

What they promise: Anonymous surveys where reviewers can speak honestly without fear.

What users experience: "There are concerns about the anonymity of feedback, with some users feeling that their comments can be traced back to them, leading to potential retaliatory actions." (Culture Amp users)

The Problem: Policy-based anonymity requires trusting the platform, its employees, and its security. When employees fear being traced, honest feedback disappears—even if the platform never actually looks.

❌ Promise #2: "Transparent, Predictable Pricing"

What they promise: Simple per-user pricing that scales with your team.

What users experience: "If you add a single employee for a single day, they will bill you immediately. Absolutely no refund." (15Five) • "20% renewal increase after multi-year agreement. Refused relief until we involved VP of Finance." (Culture Amp) • "$11/user becomes $25/user with necessary modules." (Lattice)

The Problem: Per-user pricing creates perverse incentives. Platforms bill immediately for adds but refuse credits for reductions. Renewal prices jump 15-20%. Module costs compound. What looks like $9/user becomes $120-160/user/year.

❌ Promise #3: "Your Data Is Secure"

What they promise: Enterprise-grade security for your sensitive performance data.

What users experience: "An hour of writing, hit submit, page refreshes—all work lost." (Culture Amp) • "Rippling integration creates duplicate profiles and terrible data." (Culture Amp) • "Can't map ADP fields, must create custom fields." (Lattice)

The Problem: When your data lives on vendor servers with closed-source code, you can't verify security claims. Integration failures corrupt data. Timeouts delete work. You have no control, no visibility, no recourse.

How Blik Actually Delivers on These Promises

✓ Cryptographic Anonymity (Not Policy-Based)

The Technical Approach

  • UUID tokens: Random, unguessable identifiers
  • Email separation: Reviewer emails never linked to responses
  • Database architecture: Token-response mapping stored separately from content
  • Admin-proof: Even system admins cannot identify reviewers

Why This Matters

You can tell your team: "Even I can't see who wrote what." That's not a policy—it's a technical guarantee. Open source means they can verify it themselves. This eliminates fear, which is the prerequisite for honest feedback.

Psychological safety isn't about trust—it's about technical impossibility. We architected Blik so tracing reviewers is impossible.
