The Anonymity Difference

Both platforms promise anonymous feedback. But there's a crucial difference between "we promise not to look" and "it's technically impossible to identify reviewers."

Blik's Cryptographic Anonymity

  • UUID tokens: Random, unguessable identifiers
  • Email separation: Reviewer emails never linked to responses
  • Admin-proof: Even system admins cannot identify reviewers
  • GDPR compliant: Built-in data minimization
  • Open source: Audit the code yourself

Technical guarantee: It's impossible to trace feedback to individuals.

Lattice's Platform Anonymity

  • Platform promise: "We won't look at who said what"
  • Data centralization: All data stored on Lattice servers
  • Policy-based: Trust in company policies
  • Closed source: No way to verify claims
  • Third-party risk: Lattice staff have access

Policy-based: You must trust the platform and its employees.

Why This Matters

360° feedback only works when reviewers feel psychologically safe. Research shows that perceived anonymity isn't enough—employees are more honest when they know it's technically impossible to identify them. With Blik, you can tell your team: "Even I can't see who wrote what." That's the trust foundation that makes feedback valuable.
