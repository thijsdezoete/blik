The Data Loss Problem

The most severe UX failure in performance review software: platforms that delete your work without warning.

Real Culture Amp User Experience

"I hate this platform for one simple reason, it loves to delete your work. Picture this, you spend an hour writing a glorious review for a co-worker. You're finally happy with it and you hit submit! Does it submit though? No. Culture-Amp decides that the submit button should instead refresh the page (some un-alerted timeout feature), and all your work is lost."

— Trustpilot Review, Culture Amp User

Blik's Protection

  • Auto-save functionality: Work saved as you type
  • No silent timeouts: Session management is transparent
  • Draft recovery: Come back and resume anytime
  • Clear warnings: If session expires, you're notified before data loss
  • Browser persistence: Data cached locally as backup

Your thoughtful feedback is too valuable to lose. We protect every word.

Culture Amp's Risk

  • Silent timeouts: No warning before session expires
  • Submit = Refresh: Submit button can reload page and lose work
  • No auto-save: Must manually save or risk losing everything
  • Hours lost: Users report losing hour+ of thoughtful feedback
  • Repeat occurrence: Happens multiple times per year

One timeout can destroy morale and waste hours of thoughtful work.

Why This Matters

Performance reviews require deep thought. When employees spend 30-60 minutes crafting thoughtful, constructive feedback only to have it disappear, several things happen: (1) They lose trust in the platform, (2) They rush through rewrites with less thoughtful content, (3) They become resentful of the review process itself. Data loss is not just a UX bug—it's a fundamental breach of trust that corrupts the entire feedback culture.

The Anonymity Trust Gap

Culture Amp Users Don't Trust Anonymity

"There are concerns about the anonymity of feedback, with some users feeling that their comments can be traced back to them, leading to potential retaliatory actions."

— Multiple user reviews aggregated

Blik's Cryptographic Anonymity

  • UUID tokens: Random, unguessable identifiers
  • Email separation: Reviewer emails never linked to responses
  • Admin-proof: Even system admins cannot identify reviewers
  • Technical guarantee: Architecture makes tracing impossible
  • Open source verification: Audit the anonymity code yourself
  • GDPR compliant: Data minimization by design

Tell your team: "Even I can't see who wrote what." That's true psychological safety.

Culture Amp's Policy-Based Anonymity

  • Platform promise: "We won't reveal who said what"
  • Closed source: Can't verify anonymity claims
  • User fear: "Comments can be traced back"
  • Retaliatory concerns: Employees worry about consequences
  • Trust required: Must believe platform and all staff
  • Data centralization: All feedback on Culture Amp servers

When employees fear being traced, honest feedback disappears.

The Honesty Tax

360° feedback only works when reviewers feel safe being honest. Research shows that perceived anonymity isn't enough—employees are more candid when they know it's technically impossible to identify them. Culture Amp users explicitly report anonymity concerns. With Blik's cryptographic approach, you eliminate that fear entirely. The difference isn't theoretical—it's the difference between getting honest feedback and getting politically safe platitudes.
