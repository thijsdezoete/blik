Shared Philosophy: Simplicity Over Feature Bloat

Both tools avoid the "do everything badly" trap of enterprise HR platforms. Keep it simple, do it well.

Where we differ: Small Improvements is lightweight SaaS. Blik is lightweight and open source. Same idea about simplicity, different control model.

If you want Small Improvements' philosophy but with self-hosting, that's basically what Blik is.

What Blik Does

  • 360° multi-rater feedback
  • Anonymous peer reviews
  • Role-specific questionnaires
  • Automated people analytics
  • Performance insights

What Blik doesn't do: OKRs, 1-on-1s, continuous feedback, engagement surveys. Use other tools for those.

What Small Improvements Does

  • 360° feedback cycles
  • 1-on-1 meeting notes
  • Objectives & key results
  • Continuous feedback
  • Praise & recognition

Broader scope: Covers more of the continuous performance management workflow.

Feature Comparison

360° Feedback Cycles

Blik:

4 pre-built questionnaires (software engineering, manager, professional, quick). Cryptographic anonymity. Automated Dreyfus skill assessment.

Small Improvements:

Flexible feedback cycles with custom questions. Anonymous option. Manual review process.

Continuous Feedback

Blik:

Not included. Focused on structured 360° review cycles only. Integrate with Slack/Teams for ad-hoc feedback.

Small Improvements:

Built-in praise, feedback requests, and continuous feedback between cycles. Core feature.

1-on-1 Meetings

Blik:

Not included. Use your preferred tool (Notion, Google Docs, etc.) for meeting notes.

Small Improvements:

Shared meeting notes, talking points, action items. Integrated with feedback and objectives.

Analytics & Insights

Blik:

Automated people analytics: Dreyfus competency model, perception gap analysis, peer benchmarks. AI-driven insights.

Small Improvements:

Reports on feedback completion, ratings trends, objectives progress. Manual interpretation required.

Which Should You Choose?

Choose Blik If...

  • You want self-hosting capability and data ownership
  • You prefer open source over proprietary software
  • You need flat-rate or free pricing (no per-user costs)
  • 360° feedback is your primary need
  • You want automated people analytics (Dreyfus model)
  • True cryptographic anonymity matters
  • You're comfortable without 1-on-1s/OKRs in same tool
Try Blik

Choose Small Improvements If...

  • You want an all-in-one lightweight performance tool
  • 1-on-1s and continuous feedback are important
  • You need objectives/goals tracking integrated
  • You prefer fully-managed SaaS (no self-hosting)
  • You're okay with per-user pricing
  • Standard "anonymous" surveys are sufficient
  • You want a more comprehensive feature set
