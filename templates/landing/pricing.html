Pricing Questions

Why is self-hosting free?

Blik is open source under AGPL-3.0 license. I built this because I was tired of paying €15,000/year for features I didn't need. If you can host it yourself, you should be able to use it for free. The hosted plans exist for teams that want the software without the DevOps overhead.

What's the catch with €49/month?

No catch. It's a flat €49/month for up to 50 employees, all features included. I wanted pricing that doesn't require a spreadsheet to calculate. If you grow past 50 employees, you move to the €199/month tier for up to 200 employees. That's it.

Are there any hidden costs?

None. €49 is €49. No setup fees, no per-user charges, no feature restrictions, no surprise renewal increases. The only way your bill goes up is if you grow past 50 employees and need the larger plan.

What happens if I go over 50 employees on the €49 plan?

Nothing immediate. We're not going to shut you down at employee #51. When you're ready, you can upgrade to the €199 plan which covers up to 200 employees. We'll send you a reminder when you're approaching the limit.

Can I switch from self-hosted to hosted later?

Yes, but we don't have an automated migration tool yet. You'd need to export your data from your self-hosted instance and we can help you import it into a hosted account. If this is something you need, reach out and we'll figure it out.

Do you offer discounts for non-profits or education?

Not formally yet, but reach out. For non-profits and educational institutions, I'm happy to work something out. Self-hosting is always free regardless.

What payment methods do you accept?

Credit card via Stripe for hosted plans. We handle EUR, USD, and GBP. Invoicing is available for annual contracts on the Enterprise plan.

Is there a refund policy?

14-day free trial means you can try before you pay. After that, if you cancel, you're billed for the current month and that's it. No refunds on partial months, but you keep access until the end of your billing period.

How does pricing compare to building this in-house?

A junior developer for 2-3 months would cost you more than 5 years of Blik hosting. Plus you'd own the maintenance burden forever. Self-hosting Blik costs you maybe 4 hours of DevOps time for initial setup, then it just runs.
